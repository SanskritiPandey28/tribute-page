Dr A.P.J Abdul Kalam

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Timeline of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's Life

  • 1931 Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a homemaker.
  • 1940s Developed a strong interest in science and mathematics during his school years. Worked as a newspaper vendor to support his education.
  • 1950 Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1954-1958 Completed Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). Designed a low-cost hovercraft as a student project.
  • 1960 Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist. Worked on designing helicopters for the Indian Army.
  • 1969 Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Played a key role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3, which successfully launched the Rohini Satellite in 1980.
  • 1970s-1980s Developed ballistic missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP). Contributed to the development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."
  • 1998 Played a crucial role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran-II, making India a nuclear-armed state.
  • 2002-2007 Served as the 11th President of India, widely loved as the "People's President." Advocated for education, innovation, and youth empowerment during his tenure.
  • 2002-2015 Authored several inspirational books, including: “Wings of Fire” (Autobiography). “Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India.” “India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium.”
  • 2015 Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong. Left behind a legacy of inspiration, scientific achievement, and dedication to nation-building.

Legacy Highlights

Received India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, in 1997, along with the Padma Bhushan (1981) and Padma Vibhushan (1990). His vision, “Transforming India into a developed nation by 2020,” continues to inspire millions. Remembered for his simplicity, humility, and relentless focus on empowering youth through education.
